# Business Case

FinPlanIQ simulates the FP&A reporting process for a mid-sized technology company with multiple revenue streams, geographic regions, and operating departments.

## Company Context

The simulated company generates revenue from four business units:

- Enterprise Subscription
- SMB Subscription
- Professional Services
- Data & Insights

The company operates across four regions:

- North America
- Europe
- APAC
- LATAM

Operating expenses are tracked across six departments:

- Sales
- Marketing
- Engineering
- Product
- Customer Success
- G&A

## FP&A Use Case

The finance team needs to prepare a monthly business review for leadership. The key questions are:

- Did revenue meet budget?
- Which business units or regions underperformed?
- Which departments exceeded their operating expense budget?
- How did revenue and expense variance impact EBITDA?
- What is the forecasted outlook for the next six months?
- What happens to profitability under downside scenarios?

## Key Assumptions

- Revenue grows over time with monthly variability.
- Certain months include planned underperformance to create realistic variance analysis.
- COGS varies by business unit.
- Payroll is driven by department headcount.
- Operating expenses include payroll and other department-level spend.
- Marketing and Engineering include intentional overspend periods to simulate real business pressure.
- Forecasting uses recent historical trends rather than complex machine learning.
- Scenario analysis focuses on common FP&A questions: revenue decline, payroll inflation, and marketing spend changes.

## Why This Matters

This project mirrors the type of analysis finance teams perform during monthly close, budget reviews, forecast updates, and leadership reporting. It combines financial analysis, SQL reporting, Python data workflows, dashboarding, and business storytelling.
